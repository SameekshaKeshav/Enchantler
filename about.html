Engage

Source dataset:

  • Its a sample dataset provided for the microsoft engage mentorship program.

  • The dataset is about availability of model and variants from the manufacturers.

  • Lot of attributes and classifications of the make models also available in this dataset.

  • The various attributes present in the dataset were:

    1. Make: The manufacturer of the specific car is specified.
    2. Model: The model of the car under the manufacturer is specified in the coulumn.
    3. Variant: The variant indicates the type of vehicle as well as various attributes.
    4. Showroom Price: The present showroom price of the model is specified.
    5. Fuel type: The type of fuel the engine takes is mentioned in this column.
    6. Body type: The type of vehicle for example Hatchback SUV are mentioned in this column.

Use cases:

  • Availability vs Price:

    1. Describes the availability of models i.e. the number of models present against price range.
    2. This will give a glimpse of price range that market and consumers are focussing on.
    3. Its always a good indication that represents the prosperity levels in the population.

  • Availability by Manufacturer:

    1. Can be used to identify highly invested manufacturer in research and development which is indicated by the wide variety of offerings in the market.

  • Selector by Price:

    1. Helps users to filter out the cars available in a specific price range.
    2. End user can explore models and varients available for the budget on his mind.
    3. Can be further extended to include filtering by other attributes.

Target dataset:

  • Columns required were identified and only these columns were pulled from the source dataset.

  • The various attributes present in the target dataset are:

    1. Make: The manufacturer of the specific car is specified.
    2. Model: The model of the car under the manufacturer is specified in this coulumn.
    3. Variant: The variant indicates the type of vehicle as well as various attributes.
    4. Showroom Price: The present showroom price of the model is converted to is nearby value.

  • Price range was identified as a computed attribute required for the implementation of the use case.

Transformation:

  • The computation of price range was implemented in python using the pandas dataframe.

EVVehicles

Source dataset:

  • It was a dataset procured from ihsmarkit.com.

  • It talks about the registration of electric vehicles across the country of USA.

  • Lot of attributes and classifications of the make models accross different states of USA are also available in this dataset.

  • The various attributes present are:

    1. STATE: Specifies a state in the USA where different MakeModels are listed.
    2. MAKE: The manufacturer of the car model is specified.
    3. MODEL: The model of the car under the manufacturer is specified in this coulumn.
    4. EV_VEHICLES: The number of electric vehicles registered in the particular state is recorderded under this column.
    5. MILES/kWh: The number of miles the car can run per kWh is specified in this column.

Use cases:

  • Electric Vehicles by Manufacturer:

    1. Can be used to identify the manufacturer ,whose cars are maximum registered across all the states in USA.

  • Electric Vehicles vs State:

    1. A pie chart where area of sector is analogus to number of electric vehicles present in the state is painted on the screen.
    2. Can be used the identify the most green state by looking at the largest sector hence identifing the most electric vehicles containing state.

Target dataset:

  • The given state names were translated to a 2-letter state code.

  • The various attributes present in the target dataset are:

    1. STATE: Specifies a state in the USA where different MakeModels are listed.
    2. STATE CODE: Specifies a 2-letter state code defined for each state in the USA.
    3. MAKE: The manufacturer of the car model is specified.
    4. MODEL: The model of the car under the manufacturer is specified in this coulumn.
    5. EV_VEHICLES: The number of electric vehicles registered in the particular state is recorderded under this column.
    6. MILES/kWh: The number of miles the car can run per kWh is specified in this column.

  • EV_VEHICLES was identified as a computed attribute for the implementation of use case.

Transformation:

  • The given state names were translatted to a 2-letter state code using the VLOOKUP feature of excel.
  • The computation of range of EV_VEHICLES was implemented in python using the pandas dataframe.

Car Sales

Source dataset:

  • It was procured from public domain.

  • It tells us about the sales for a particular model given specific attributes of the car.

  • Lot of attributes and classifications of the make models along with its sales are available in this dataset.

  • The various attributes present are:

    1. Manufacturer: The manufacturer for the model is specified in this column
    2. Model: The model of the car is specified in this dataset
    3. Sales in thousand: The sales of the car model divided by 1000 is mentioned in this column
    4. Price in thousands: The price of the car in thousand USD is mentioned in this column
    5. Horsepower: The energy of the engine in terms of horsepower is mentioned in this column
    6. Curb Weight: The default weight of the car is specified in this column
    7. Fuel efficiency: How efficiently the fuel is used can be determined from this column
    8. Power perf factor: Indicates the performance of the engine compares to the power

Use cases:

  • Sales vs horsepower:

    1. Helps in understanding the range of horsepower mostly bought by people across USA.
    2. It helps in determining if horsepower is given preferance or the price as greater the hopsepower greater the price.

  • Sales vs Curb weight:

    1. Helps us determine if people prefer larger cars to smaller cars or vice versa.
    2. The curb weight is one of the basic representation of the size of car.

  • Sales vs Fuel efficiency:

    1. Helps us determine if fuel efficiency is a factor that determines sales of a car.
    2. Fuel efficiency is a technical detail not known to every common man.

  • Sales vs Price:

    1. Helps us analyse the price range that an average human being spends on his car across different states of USA.

  • Sales by manufacturer:

    1. Helps us identify succesful manufacturer in the market.

Target dataset:

  • The required columns were pulled from original dataset

  • The various attributes present in the target dataset are:

    1. Manufacturer: The manufacturer for the model is specified in this column
    2. Sales in thousand: The sales of the car model divided by 1000 is mentioned in this column
    3. Price in thousands: The price of the car in thousand USD is mentioned in this column
    4. Horsepower: The energy of the engine in terms of horsepower is mentioned in this column
    5. Curb Weight: The default weight of the car is specified in this column
    6. Fuel efficiency: How efficiently the fuel is used can be determined from this column
    7. Power perf factor: Indicates the performance of the engine compares to the power

  • Computed attributes for implementation of use cases:

    1. Curb weight
    2. Horsepower
    3. Fuel_efficiency

Transformation:

The initial sales dataset is made to run through a python script where the following changes are made:

  • The model and the latest launch attributes are dropped
  • The records with missing valus are removed
  • If any unknown values are present the the record is removed
  • The values of:
    1. Curb weight were rounded in terms of 0.5's.
    2. Horsepower were rounded in terms of 50's.
    3. Fuel efficiency were rounded in terms of 2's.
